Steve Pennells is an Australian writer and producer and one of the country’s most awarded and influential journalists. A five-time Walkley Award winner, including the Gold Walkley, he spent more than two decades reporting from war zones, political flashpoints and the frontlines of social injustice with a reputation for urgent, human-driven storytelling that shaped national conversations across print, television and documentary.

His work has been recognised with a record six UN Media Peace Prizes and the Clarion Award for Most Outstanding Contribution to Journalism. He was named by MediaWeek as one of the top 25 people working in Australian media, and in 2018 became the youngest living inductee into the Australian Media Hall of Fame, where he was described as “one of Australia’s most influential journalists of the past two decades across print, television, photo and video journalism.”

In recent years, Pennells has transitioned into scripted film and television through Deadset Pictures, developing premium drama that interrogates the same questions that defined his journalism: who holds power, who stays silent, and what people justify in order to survive. His work is characterised by moral ambiguity, layered timelines, and an acute focus on reputation versus truth, often centring women navigating systems designed to consume them. He is currently developing multiple scripted projects, including the prestige drama Stiletto.
